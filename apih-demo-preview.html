Decision Brief · Sample — Reference Case

Structural stress at a fuel-critical facility — what ground sensors could not see.

Classification: Client ConfidentialDelivered: SMS alert + signed PDF
The question

"We depend on a single refinery for regional fuel supply. Is there any early structural signal on the facility we should know about before it reaches the news?"

Why ground telemetry misses it

Refineries rely on point-source sensors — gas sniffers, pressure gauges, control-room telemetry — each reading one location. As a process unit drifts toward failure, stress first shows at facility scale: millimetric ground deformation and a persistent thermal shift across the unit's footprint — often before point sensors localise the cause.

The evidence — from orbit
  • SARRadar interferometry penetrates dust, cloud, darkness — reads ground and structural deformation to 3–5 mm against a 6–12 month baseline.
  • LWIRThermal imaging flags persistent heat anomalies at unit footprint scale — zones, not single components.
  • ΔChange-detection logic isolates genuine structural deviation from weather noise; human-in-the-loop triage validates before anything is sent.
The read — as it would have appeared
Alert · High Priority

"Ground deformation 6 mm + thermal drift across Crude Unit Zone B footprint · persistent 72-hour shift vs baseline. Recommended action: inspect within operational shift."

In the real June 2026 event, a pressure drop was detected on control boards only moments before the fire. The 13-hour blaze cost roughly 150 million litres of diesel — from a facility producing ~10% of national fuel.

What you'd watch next

Three named indicators, checked on cadence — the early tells that the read is strengthening or fading — plus which of your sites and contracts feel a supply gap first.

Confidence & sourcing

Calibrated confidence stated on every brief

Every brief carries its sources and a confidence level. Insight, never prediction — surface indicators read for what they mean, never a guarantee.
